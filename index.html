Hiring an electrician — do this first
1
Check they're licensed. In Singapore, permanent electrical work must legally be done by an EMA Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW). You can verify any worker's licence — free — on EMA's official ELISE portal (elise.ema.gov.sg).
2
Right grade for the job. Most HDB and condo homes are covered by a Grade L7 (Licensed Electrician) — sufficient for rewiring, DB upgrades, power points, lighting and water-heater connections. Larger commercial loads need L8 or L9.
3
Get it in writing. Ask for an itemised quote, confirm whether testing/certification is included, and check the workmanship warranty before work starts.

Frequently asked
Is it legal to do my own electrical work in Singapore?
No — any permanent wiring or installation (power points, switches, lighting, the DB, rewiring) must be done by an EMA-licensed worker. Everyday things like plugging in appliances or changing a bulb are fine.
How do I check my electrician is actually licensed?
Search their name or licence on EMA's free ELISE portal (elise.ema.gov.sg). A legitimate electrician will give you their licence details without hesitation.
What licence grade do I need for an HDB or condo?
For standard homes, a Grade L7 (Licensed Electrician) covers the work — rewiring, DB upgrades, points and lighting. Bigger commercial/industrial loads need L8 or L9.
Why does my power keep tripping?
Usually an overloaded circuit or a faulty appliance — unplug recent appliances and reset the tripped switch once. If it trips again immediately or repeatedly, stop forcing it on and get it checked; it can signal a wiring fault.
How long does a full HDB rewiring take?
Typically about 1–3 days depending on flat size, number of points, and whether the wiring is surface or concealed. Your electrician should confirm the timeline in the quote.
