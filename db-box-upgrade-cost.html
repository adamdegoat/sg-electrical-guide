DB box upgrade: what it costs, and when you need one

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service 2026 pricing · indicative

Your DB box (the distribution board — that grey box of switches) is the heart of your home's electrics. When it's old or undersized, everything downstream suffers. Here's when it's worth upgrading and what it should cost.

What the DB box actually does

It splits your incoming supply into separate circuits — lights, sockets, kitchen, aircon — and protects each one. A modern board has individual MCBs (circuit breakers) plus an RCCB, the safety switch that cuts power instantly if it senses a leak to earth. Older flats sometimes still have the old rewireable fuse type with no RCCB at all — which is the main reason to upgrade.

Signs yours needs an upgrade

  • Old-style rewireable fuses, or no RCCB / ELCB at all
  • Breakers that trip more and more often
  • You're adding load — a new aircon, water heater, oven or a kitchen reno
  • Burn marks, a buzzing sound, or a warm board
  • Not enough spare ways to add the circuits you need

What it costs in Singapore

HDB / condo DB upgrade$350 – $800
Larger home / more circuits$800 – $1,200+
Landed / penthouse (many circuits)$1,000 – $1,500+

Indicative 2026 ranges, depending on board size, number of breakers, brand and testing.

✓ What a proper job includes

A good DB upgrade isn't just swapping the box — it should come with a correctly-rated RCCB, clearly labelled circuits, and the whole board tested afterward. Make sure that's in the quote.
