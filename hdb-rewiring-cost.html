How much does it cost to rewire an HDB flat?

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service 2026 pricing · indicative

Short answer: for a full HDB rewiring you're usually looking at somewhere between $1,800 and $6,000, depending mostly on your flat size and how many points you have. Here's the honest breakdown so you know what's fair.

3-room flat$1,800 – $3,500
4-room flat$2,500 – $4,500
5-room / executive$3,000 – $6,000

Indicative 2026 ranges. Your real number depends on the points below — always get an itemised quote.

What actually moves the price

  • Concealed vs surface wiring. Running new cables inside the wall (concealed) looks clean but costs more than surface trunking. This is usually the biggest swing.
  • Number of points. More power points, light points and switches = more labour and material. A flat with lots of added points costs more than a bare one.
  • Age & condition. Older flats with brittle original wiring or awkward conduit take longer.
  • Fittings & brand. Branded sockets and switches (Schneider, MK and the like) add a bit per point.
  • Whether the DB box is included. Many rewiring jobs also replace the old DB — factor that in.

Does your flat actually need rewiring?

You don't rewire just because a flat is old. But it's worth doing if you tick a few of these:

  • The flat is 25–30+ years old and still on its original wiring.
  • Breakers trip often, or you still have the old-style rewireable fuses instead of an RCCB.
  • You're renovating anyway — far cheaper to rewire before the carpentry and tiling go in.
  • Sockets are scorched, warm, or you smell burning.
✓ A quick tip before you commit

Get two or three itemised quotes, and check that testing and certification are included — a proper rewiring should end with the circuits tested and signed off, not just cables pulled. And make sure whoever does it is EMA-licensed (it's the law here).

If you're mid-renovation, tell your electrician your plans for each room — where the TV, study desk, kitchen appliances and aircon will go — so they put points where you'll actually use them. Adding one later costs far more than including it now.
