How to choose a good electrician in Singapore

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service Reviewed by a Licensed Electrical Worker

The cheapest quote isn't the goal — a job that's safe, lasts, and won't void your insurance is. Here's a short, practical checklist for picking someone you can trust with your home's wiring.

1. Check the licence first — on ELISE

Before anything else, confirm they're an EMA Licensed Electrical Worker. You can search any name or licence number for free on the official elise.ema.gov.sg portal. For a normal HDB or condo, a Grade L7 covers the work. Anyone who hesitates to give you their licence is a no.

2. Get an itemised, written quote

Not just a single number on WhatsApp. A proper quote lists the points, the fittings, and whether testing and certification are included. That's how you compare fairly and avoid "oh, that's extra" surprises mid-job.

3. Ask these quick questions

  • Is testing and a completion check included?
  • Is there a workmanship warranty, and how long?
  • Have you done this exact type of job before? (photos of past work help)
  • For bigger jobs — can you handle any required submissions?
⚠ Red flags

A price far below everyone else, no licence offered, cash-only with no paperwork, or a "handyman" who says they'll "just do the wiring quietly." If the work isn't done by a licensed worker and something goes wrong later, your insurance can refuse the claim — the savings vanish fast.

4. Reliability matters as much as price

Especially for renovations, an electrician who turns up when they say and finishes on time saves you far more than a few dollars off. Reviews, referrals from your ID firm or neighbours, and years in business are all good signs.
