Why are my lights flickering?

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service Reviewed by a Licensed Electrical Worker

Most flickering is harmless and easy to fix — usually an LED that doesn't like its dimmer. But a couple of patterns are worth paying attention to, because they can point to a loose connection, which you don't want to ignore.

The usual harmless causes

  • LED bulb + old dimmer. Easily the most common one in Singapore homes now. Many LEDs flicker on dimmers built for the old incandescent bulbs. Swap to a dimmable LED or a compatible dimmer and it stops.
  • A bulb that's slightly loose in its holder, or simply on its way out.
  • A cheap or failing LED driver inside the fitting.

The ones worth taking seriously

  • Lights dim whenever a big appliance kicks in (aircon, water heater, washing machine). An occasional small dip is normal; a noticeable, regular dim can mean the circuit is overloaded or a connection is loose.
  • Several lights flicker together, or a whole room does — that points to the circuit or the DB, not one bulb.
  • A switch that buzzes or feels warm, or flickering with a faint burning smell. Stop and get it checked — a loose connection can overheat.

What you can check yourself

  1. Is it one light or many? One = probably the bulb or fitting. Many = the circuit.
  2. Try a different (dimmable) bulb in the flickering fitting.
  3. Note whether it flickers on its own or only when something else switches on.
⚠ When to call someone

If multiple lights flicker, a switch is warm or buzzing, or the dimming tracks your aircon turning on — get a licensed electrician to check the connections. Loose wiring is the one cause of flickering that can quietly become a fire risk.
