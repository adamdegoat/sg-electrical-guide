No power in one room — what to check

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service Reviewed by a Licensed Electrical Worker

When the rest of the flat is fine but one room (or a couple of sockets) has gone dead, it's good news, really — it means one specific circuit has cut out, not your whole supply. Here's how to narrow it down.

Start at the DB box

Your home is split into separate circuits, each with its own switch in the DB. Nine times out of ten, "one room is dead" just means that room's breaker has tripped. Open the DB and look for a switch that's flipped down or sitting in the middle while the others are up.

  • If you find one down, flip it firmly back up once.
  • If it stays up and power returns — done. Something briefly overloaded it.
  • If it trips again immediately, leave it off. That circuit has a fault — time for a professional.

If nothing's tripped in the DB

If all the breakers look normal but the room's still dead, a few other things could be going on:

  • A single dead socket — could be a faulty point or a loose connection at that outlet.
  • A loose connection somewhere on the circuit — often where wires join behind a socket or switch.
  • An aircon or water-heater isolator switched off (check any wall switches in that area).
A quick check

Plug something you know works (like a phone charger) into a few different sockets in that room. If all are dead it's the circuit; if just one is dead it's that point. Either way, opening up sockets or chasing the wiring is licensed-electrician work in Singapore — not a DIY job.

A loose or burnt connection is the one to take seriously — it can heat up over time. If a breaker won't stay on, or a socket is dead with no obvious reason, get it traced properly rather than living with an extension cord run from the next room.
