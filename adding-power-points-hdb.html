Adding power points in your HDB

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service 2026 pricing · indicative

Run out of sockets? You can absolutely add more — almost everyone does after moving in. A licensed electrician has to do the work, but it's a small, common job. Here's what to expect.

Surface vs concealed

There are two ways to run the new wiring:

  • Surface (trunking). The cable runs in a neat plastic casing along the wall. Cheaper and quicker, and easy to add later. You'll see the trunking, but it's tidy.
  • Concealed. The cable is buried in the wall for an invisible finish. Looks best, but it means hacking and patching — so it really only makes sense during a renovation.

✓ Plan before you drill

Think about where you'll actually use power: beside the bed for phones, at the study desk, the kitchen counter, near the TV console, and a dedicated point for each aircon. Adding a couple of extra points while the electrician is already there is far cheaper than calling them back later for one.

One thing to watch: piling everything onto one circuit with multi-plugs and extension cords is how you end up with a tripping (or worse) problem. If you're adding a lot, your electrician may suggest a new circuit from the DB — worth it for heavy users like the kitchen.
