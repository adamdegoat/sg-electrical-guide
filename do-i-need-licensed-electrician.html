Do I need a licensed electrician?

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service Reviewed by a Licensed Electrical Worker

For almost any real electrical work in Singapore — yes, and it's not just good advice, it's the law. Permanent wiring has to be done by a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW) registered with the Energy Market Authority (EMA).

What legally needs a licensed worker

If the job involves opening up the wiring, making a permanent connection, or touching the DB box, it needs an LEW:

  • Adding or moving a power point or light switch
  • Installing or relocating lighting and ceiling fans
  • Changing or upgrading the DB / fuse box
  • Rewiring a room or the whole flat
  • Wiring up a water heater, oven or aircon point

What you can do yourself

Everyday stuff is fine — no licence needed:

  • Plugging in and unplugging appliances
  • Changing a light bulb
  • Resetting a switch in the DB after it trips (once)
⚠ The "handyman" shortcut isn't worth it

Getting an unlicensed handyman to add a few points to save money is a real risk — if something goes wrong later (a fire, a shock), your insurance can refuse to pay out because the work wasn't done legally. It's also just not safe. The savings aren't worth it.

How to check your electrician is actually licensed

This is the bit most people skip. You can verify any electrician for free on EMA's official ELISE portal (elise.ema.gov.sg) — search their name or licence number. A legitimate electrician will hand over their details without blinking.

You'll also see a grade:

Grade L7 — Licensed ElectricianMost HDB & condo work
Grade L8 — Licensed Electrical TechnicianLarger commercial loads
Grade L9 — Licensed Electrical EngineerHeavy / high-voltage

For a normal home, a Grade L7 covers everything you'll need — rewiring, DB upgrades, points and lighting.
