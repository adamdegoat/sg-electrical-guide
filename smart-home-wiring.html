Smart switches & home wiring

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service General guide

Smart lighting and switches are great — until you open up the wall and discover the one thing most older Singapore homes are missing. Here's the catch nobody mentions before you buy.

The neutral-wire catch

Most smart switches need a neutral wire at the switch box to power their electronics. The thing is, plenty of older HDB and condo switch boxes were wired with only the live (switched) wire — no neutral. So the smart switch you bought may not have anything to connect to.

Options when there's no neutral: buy smart switches specifically designed to work without a neutral, or have an electrician run a neutral to that box. Which is possible depends on your wiring — worth checking before you buy a whole house's worth.

What you can do yourself vs not

  • Plug-in smart stuff — smart plugs, bulbs, a hub — no electrician needed, just plug and pair.
  • Swapping a hard-wired switch — that's working on the wiring, which in Singapore must be done by a licensed electrician.

Planning a smarter home

  • Decide what you actually want automated — lights, fans, aircon, curtains — before wiring.
  • Pick one ecosystem (so everything talks to each other) before buying piecemeal.
  • If you're renovating, this is the time to run neutrals to switch points and add the wiring for motorised curtains or sensors.
✓ Doing a reno? Plan it now

Adding neutrals and extra switch wiring is cheap while the walls are open and almost always regretted later. Tell your electrician you want a "smart-ready" setup and they can wire for it from the start.
