Why does my power keep tripping?

⚡ Evergrow Electrical Service Reviewed by a Licensed Electrical Worker

If your power keeps cutting out and you're standing in the dark fishing for the DB box — relax. Nine times out of ten it's one of three ordinary things, and your flat is actually doing exactly what it's supposed to.

That switch that "tripped" in your DB box (the consumer unit) isn't broken. It flipped on purpose. Either an MCB (the circuit breaker) sensed too much current, or the RCCB (the big main safety switch) sensed electricity leaking where it shouldn't — and it cut the power in a fraction of a second to keep you safe. So the question isn't "why did it trip", it's "what set it off."

The three usual culprits

  • An overloaded circuit. Too many hungry appliances on the same line — kettle, iron, microwave, water heater all going at once. The breaker says "too much" and trips. Very common in older flats with fewer circuits.
  • A faulty appliance. One specific thing has developed a fault — often a water heater, kettle, fridge, washing machine or an old extension cord. The moment it kicks in, the power drops.
  • A wiring or earth fault. Moisture getting into a point, ageing wiring, or a damaged cable causing a small leak to earth. This is the one worth taking seriously, especially in flats that are 20–30 years old or when it only trips after rain.

What to do right now

  1. Open the DB box and note which switch flipped down (or sits in the middle).
  2. Unplug whatever you'd just switched on, plus anything that looks old or suspect.
  3. Flip the tripped switch firmly back up once.
  4. If it holds — it was probably that appliance. Don't plug the suspect one back in until it's checked.
  5. If it trips again straight away, or trips again later with nothing obvious plugged in — stop there.
⚠ Please don't do this

Don't keep flipping the switch back on over and over, and never tape it up or bypass it to "make it stay." That switch is the one thing standing between you and a shock or a fire. If it keeps tripping, it's telling you something — let someone check it.

When to call a licensed electrician

Get it looked at if: it keeps tripping even with everything unplugged, it trips whenever it rains, there's a burning or fishy smell near a socket or the DB, a switch or socket feels warm, or your flat is old and still on the original wiring. Any of these can mean an earth fault or failing wiring — not a DIY job, and in Singapore it legally has to be done by a licensed worker anyway.

Honestly, a proper fault-finding visit usually costs around $60–$150 and saves you the guesswork (and it's often waived if you go ahead with the repair).
